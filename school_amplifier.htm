The School Amplifier

Øystein Klevhus, now at Telecast Norway AS, and myself, did a project together at the end of our engineering school.  I am greatly in debt to Øystein, because he was the one who made it possible for me to go through with that school. I was far to occupied with EC, and had far too little time for school. Øystein came to me at EC with school tasks and other stuff I had to do, and in that way it was possible for me to take that school without nearly being present. 

The amplifier :  It was a very special design, fully complementary, and used matched single field-effect transistors at the input, and some very interesting output transistors, with a very low turnover point. 

I will come back later with more info on the matching of the input transistors.   Note also, the very low emitter resistors on the output stage.  This matches a later paper I did on the AES.  More on this also later.
